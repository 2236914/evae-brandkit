Section 01
Brand Direction
The Doctor Hasia app is a luxury medical editorial experience. The reference aesthetic is drawn from SKKN by Kim Kardashian (warm monochrome, stone surfaces, zero decorative colour) and Vogue magazine (editorial serif typography, full-bleed photography, generous white space, one high-impact colour hit). The result is a light-mode app that feels like opening a luxury medical magazine — not a clinic portal.

Design principle: Every screen should feel like opening a page of Vogue, not like logging into a clinic portal. Large serif display type, warm cream surfaces, full-bleed photography, and generous negative space are non-negotiable. Complexity is hidden. Clarity is the luxury.

Section 06
Photography & Imagery
Photography is the primary visual language of the app. Every image must meet these specifications. No exceptions — a wrong image destroys the editorial aesthetic.
Editorial portrait
Doctor Hasia portraits
Black and white or warm-toned editorial. Professional clinical authority. Never casual or candid.
△
Product photography
Isolated on pure white or pearl warm. No props. No lifestyle elements. The product is the subject.
Architectural interior
Editorial atmosphere
Warm sandy tones, arched forms, natural materials. Used in community and editorial hero sections.
✓ Photography do's
Full-bleed to card edges — no white padding inside image containers
16px border radius on standalone image cards
Black and white or warm editorial tone for Doctor Hasia portraits
High contrast, clean backgrounds for product shots
Architectural and spatial photography for editorial atmosphere
Treatment photography clinical but beautiful — professional environment
✗ Photography don'ts
No casual or candid photography — always professional and editorial
No brightly lit fluorescent clinic photography — too cold
No stock photography — no generic faces or scenes
No lifestyle product shots — products on white or near-black only
No images with visible white padding inside card containers
No before/after photography without explicit patient consent documented

Section 09
Do's & Don'ts
Common mistakes that break the editorial luxury aesthetic. Every member of the design and development team should review this section before building.
✓ Typography do's
Use Cormorant Garamond for all display and serif text
One display-size heading per screen maximum
Overline label always appears above headings, never below
Pull quotes in italic Cormorant Garamond with mauve left border
Prices always in Cormorant Garamond serif, never Montserrat
✗ Typography don'ts
Never use Inter, Roboto, Arial, or system fonts
Never use two display-size headings on the same screen
Never mix serif and sans at the same hierarchy level
Never use font weights above 500 anywhere in the app
Never use underlines on body text for links — use arrows instead
✓ Colour do's
Obsidian for all text and button fills
Pearl warm for all card surfaces and secondary backgrounds
Warm white for all screen backgrounds
Mauve only in the 5 defined touchpoints
Silver for all overline labels, metadata, and captions
✗ Colour don'ts
Never use teal, green, blue, or any hue other than mauve
Never use gradients on any surface or button
Never use coloured borders on cards (only silver light or obsidian)
Never use mauve on nav items, progress bars, or step indicators
Never use dark or black screen backgrounds
✓ Layout do's
Full-bleed images to card edges, 16px radius on image cards
Generous white space between all content sections
0.5px divider lines, silver light colour only
Numbered editorial lists for service menus and article lists
Pill buttons with 20px radius on all interactive elements
✗ Layout don'ts
Never add box shadows or drop shadows to any element
Never add white padding inside image containers
Never use square buttons or 0px radius buttons
Never crowd elements — white space is a design choice not a waste
Never stack two obsidian buttons next to each other on the same screen
✓ Content do's
Post titles in community written as editorial headlines, not sentences
Service descriptions in Doctor Hasia's clinical voice, not marketing copy
Product descriptions include clinical rationale and key actives
Course cards show module count and CPD hours where applicable
Contraindications section on every service detail screen
✗ Content don'ts
Never use generic marketing language on service or product descriptions
Never show before/after photography without documented patient consent
Never omit the contraindications section from service detail screens
Never use exclamation marks in copy — the brand tone is measured and expert
Never truncate overline labels — if too long, rewrite shorter
